MEng Design Project: Acoustic Localization and Tracking via Machine Learning

Abstract: For our project, our goal is to track users in a room using an array of microphones. To do this, we first model a room with echoes, and we populate this room with users and microphones. We extract information from this modelled room to create a so-called channel state information (CSI) matrix. Depending on the application, we use a supervised or an unsupervised deep learning model to learn the channel from the CSI matrix. If the goal is to predict user locations in cartesian space, we use the supervised model. If we want to learn the channel itself, we use the unsupervised model. Though a lot of research in localization has been done in RF communication, we use acoustic communication to further understand its merits, its workings, and its negatives to hopefully expand a field in ECE.

Take Me to the Streets Video Demo


What's going on?

This video demo was created as a supplement to our project presentation, which can be found here.

The purpose of this video is to show the different features our project would have, should it be implemented in the actual Hiltons app.

We start with a Hilton customer who wants to play this exploration game. The app would route her to the center of a given neighborhood (suggested by Hilton itself). Once she goes within a certain radius of the neighborhood, the routing is turned off in order to encourage exploration of local businesses. If she accidently gets lost and leaves the radius, she is routed back to the center.

Once a business is reached, the user is notified, and she would receive a certain amount of credits. After exploring that place of interest for a bit, she decides to stop playing, causing landmarks to appear on the map. This person is free to check out the rest of landmarks, or to simply go back to the Hilton.
